[OMISSIS] [ABOUT]

Omissis is a sound design experience that reflects on government and media censorship through the lens of the Epstein case, specifically, the vast trove of documents released to the public. Despite more than three million files being made available, much of the truth remains hidden behind carefully placed redactions.  The site draws on fragments pulled from this enormous archive. Using sampling and voice synthesis, these fragments, originally flat, written text, have been turned into spoken testimony, voiced through an AI reconstruction of Epstein himself. Every redaction in the original documents has been replaced with a beep, its length calculated from the natural pace of the speech around it, so that each silence feels real rather than arbitrary.  All of these synthesized fragments converge into a single composition, the project's final statement. It's meant to expose the confusion built into this release: an overwhelming quantity of material, handed over with one hand while the truth is quietly withheld with the other, censored with near-surgical precision.  Two techniques drive this composition. The first is the layering of Epstein's voice across multiple overlapping fragments, evoking the sheer scale of what's been released. The second is a gradual muffling of those same voices, a way of shifting focus onto the redactions themselves, until the silences are the only thing left that truly registers.

Developed for the "Sound Design - Alfabetizzazione sonora e cultura del suono" course, Politecnico di Milano (Communication Design, A.Y. 2025/2026).
Design & Development: Jacopo Baldessari, Diego Chinni, Simone Menoni, Giacomo Piganzoli.
Professors: Liberti Vittorio, Riva Micol
